About

It hits you in the middle of the day.

Not when you're ready for it. Not when you have space for it. When you're at your desk. Distracted. Functioning.

Then something small — her handwriting somewhere. A song. A coffee cup that looks like hers. And suddenly there's a rush. Everything gets irritating. Your eyes fill. You step out. Light a cigarette. Try to come back.

But from that moment — she's on your mind for the rest of the day. And you have nowhere to put any of it.

No one to call. Nothing to send. Just the weight of words that exist but have nowhere to go.

I built this for that moment.

Not to fix it. Not to move on. Just to have somewhere to put the words.

Because sometimes that's enough.
